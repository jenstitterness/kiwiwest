ki wi [kee-wee] noun, plural -wis.
1. Nocturnal flightless bird of New Zealand having a long neck and stout legs;
2. Informal. a New Zealander.

Welcome to Kiwi West!

Let 'kiwi' Jeanette and her husband Scott provide you a cozy nest for two on the west side of Port Angeles while you're visiting the amazing Olympic Peninsula in the Pacific Northwest. Look out the bay window and enjoy the majestic mountain view of the Olympics. Our vacation rental is attached to our large Victorian home built in 1892, but is it's own private little nest with its own fully equipped kitchen, bathroom, laundry unit and fireplace. Other comforts of home in your home-away-from-home include a TV with a VCR/DVD player, a selection of movies for your enjoyment, a porcelain claw-foot bathtub and shower, a stove and microwave are ideal for preparing your meal , and an outdoor deck with a gas grill.

We are conveniently nestled close to excellent restaurants, cafes, shopping, and close to the ferries to Victoria and the main entrance to the Olympic National Park. There are many things to keep you busy while visiting the amazing Olympic Peninsula, including exploring Hurricane Ridge, the Sol Duc Hot Springs, the Hoh Rainforest, the Pacific Northwest beaches, Tongue Point, Dungeness Scenic Loop, our Olympic Game Farm, or even a day trip to Victoria, BC . Visit our local winery, play a round of golf in one of the many golf courses, or check out the Casino for games and good music. The Pacific Northwest caters to a great deal of hobbies and interests, with the water, land, and mountains, we've got it all! Enjoy yourself while hiking, fishing, boating, kayaking, skiing, snowboarding, golfing or biking. Along the way, you may even encounter or see deer, elk, a variety of birds, shorebirds, otters, sea lions, perhaps a whale if your lucky! Guests planning on dining and shopping in the City have plenty of choices.

Thank you for looking into our cozy nest for two! We look forward to having you stay with us at Kiwi West!
